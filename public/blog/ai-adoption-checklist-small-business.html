Key Principles for Success

1. Lead with Business Impact, Not Technology

Don't say "ChatGPT will do your job." Say "ChatGPT will help you write emails 10x faster, so you can focus on client relationships." Connect to what your team actually cares about.

2. Start Small, Expand Fast

Week 1 pilots with 3-5 volunteers seem slow, but they build credibility. Week 2-3 expansion is fast because early adopters are already advocating.

3. Make It Easy (Remove Friction)

Custom GPTs, shared prompt templates, clear guidelines—these remove the "I don't know how to start" barrier. Low friction = higher adoption.

4. Celebrate Loudly

Share wins weekly. Interview power users. Show ROI numbers. Public celebration drives adoption faster than any mandate.

5. Measure Everything

Baseline metrics (Week 1), early wins (Week 2), expansion metrics (Week 3), full ROI (Week 4). Numbers build credibility with leadership and skeptical team members.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if my team doesn't hit 80% adoption by Week 3?

That's okay. Different people adopt at different paces. If you hit 50-60%, that's still progress. Focus on deepening usage with early adopters rather than forcing adoption on everyone. Laggards will follow once they see peer success.

Do we need a full-time "AI Manager"?

Not for a small team. Your AI Champion (1-2 people) can manage this 5-10 hours/week. As you scale beyond 50 people, consider a dedicated role.

What if ChatGPT isn't solving our biggest problems?

Pick different use cases. This checklist assumes you've identified high-impact use cases upfront. If you haven't, spend 1 week (before this plan) interviewing teams about their top time-sinks and pain points. Design pilots around those.

Can we do this faster than 30 days?

Technically yes, but we don't recommend it. Change management takes time. Teams need space to experiment, learn, and build confidence. Rushing leads to resistance and lower long-term adoption. 30 days is the minimum.

What about security and data privacy?

ChatGPT Business includes admin controls for data retention and sharing. Week 1, set clear guidelines: "No confidential customer data, no personal identifying information, no proprietary formulas." This is a team responsibility, not a system limitation.
