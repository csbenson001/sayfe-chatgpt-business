Update #1: GPT-5.5 Is Now the Default — What Actually Changed

As of June 12, 2026, GPT-5.2 is no longer available in ChatGPT. All users were automatically moved to GPT-5.5, which is now the standard model across ChatGPT Business.

GPT-5.5 Instant — the faster, more cost-efficient variant — was upgraded in early June with significant improvements to response quality, reduced hallucination rates (reported at 52.5% fewer in internal testing), and more consistent tone for business writing tasks.

For most ChatGPT Business users, the transition was invisible. Your chats continued without interruption. But if your team uses ChatGPT for tasks that depend on specific model behavior — legal drafting, technical analysis, structured output generation — it's worth running a few representative prompts to confirm the output quality matches your expectations under GPT-5.5.

For Custom GPT builders: If you have Custom GPTs configured with explicit model instructions calibrated to GPT-4.x behavior, review the system prompt and test outputs. GPT-5.5 is generally more capable, but instruction-following nuances differ. A 30-minute review now prevents surprises later.

Update #2: Send Emails Directly From ChatGPT (Without Switching Apps)

This is the feature that will save your team the most time per week — and it's available right now on ChatGPT Business.

If you connect Gmail or Outlook to ChatGPT, you can now draft and send emails directly from within a chat session. No copy-paste. No tab switching. No re-opening your email client. You describe what you want to send ("follow up with Sarah at Acme about the proposal, keep it professional and brief, reference our call Tuesday"), ChatGPT drafts it, you review it in the same window, and you send.

The practical applications are immediate:

  • Sales teams — follow-up emails after AI-researched prospect intel, without leaving the research thread
  • Customer support — draft and send responses while the support ticket context is active in the chat
  • Operations — vendor confirmations, internal updates, meeting summaries pushed to inboxes in one step
  • Executives — schedule your assistant-level correspondence between meetings without touching your email client

To set it up: go to ChatGPT Settings → Integrations and connect your Gmail or Outlook account. It's a one-time OAuth flow — takes about two minutes. The email integration works on the ChatGPT web app for Business, Plus, Pro, and Enterprise users.

Update #3: ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets Is Now Live Globally

ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets moved from preview to global availability for ChatGPT Business this month. If you were on a waitlist or seeing the "coming soon" message, that's gone now.

The integration puts a ChatGPT sidebar directly inside your spreadsheet. You can ask it to:

  • Explain what a formula does in plain language
  • Write complex formulas from a natural language description ("give me a formula that looks up sales rep names in column A and sums their Q2 revenue from the Transactions sheet")
  • Identify anomalies or outliers in a data range
  • Generate Python or Apps Script to automate repetitive spreadsheet tasks
  • Draft chart titles, axis labels, and summaries for presentation-ready output

For businesses running financial models, inventory tracking, client reporting, or any spreadsheet-heavy workflow, this is the integration that removes the "I'm not a spreadsheet expert" bottleneck from most of your team.

To enable it: install the ChatGPT add-in from the Microsoft AppSource (for Excel) or Google Workspace Marketplace (for Sheets). Sign in with your ChatGPT Business credentials. The sidebar appears in your spreadsheet on next open.

Update #4: New Admin Analytics and Spend Controls (June 18)

This one is for admins and business owners — but it's meaningful. OpenAI shipped an updated usage analytics dashboard for ChatGPT Business on June 18 with two key improvements:

Updated Usage Analytics

The admin panel now shows deeper per-user and per-team breakdowns of ChatGPT usage. You can see which team members are using ChatGPT most heavily, which features they're relying on, and usage trends over time. For managers trying to demonstrate AI ROI — or identify who hasn't adopted the tool yet — this data is directly useful.

Spend Controls

If your team uses ChatGPT alongside OpenAI's API (separate from the ChatGPT Business subscription), the updated spend controls let you set budget limits, configure usage alerts, and monitor API costs without digging into multiple dashboards. The controls don't apply to the ChatGPT Business subscription itself (which is a flat per-seat fee), but they're valuable for teams running any custom API integrations.

What to do: Log into your ChatGPT Business admin panel this week and explore the new Analytics section. Set up at least one usage alert so you're not surprised by anything before your next billing period.

Update #5: Memory Controls — More Granular Than Before

ChatGPT's memory feature — which lets it retain context about your preferences, work style, and ongoing projects across sessions — got more granular controls in June 2026.

You can now delete individual memories shown on your memory summary page without turning memory off entirely. Previously, the choice was binary: memory on or memory off. Now you can selectively remove specific memories that are outdated or sensitive while keeping the rest of your context intact.

To access this: go to Settings → Personalization → Memory. Click "Manage Memory" to see everything ChatGPT has retained. Click the three-dot menu next to any memory to delete it, or select "Delete and turn off memory" to start fresh entirely.

For ChatGPT Business admins: at the organizational level, your team's conversation data is still governed by the Business-tier data privacy guarantee — OpenAI does not use Business workspace data to train models. The memory controls are per-user preferences within that framework.

Update #6: OpenAI Academy — Free Training for Your Team

On June 12, OpenAI launched OpenAI Academy — a free collection of structured courses designed to help business professionals use AI more effectively at work.

The courses are structured around practical skill-building: how to write effective prompts for specific business tasks, how to use ChatGPT for data analysis, how to build Custom GPTs without coding background, and how to integrate AI into team workflows without disrupting existing processes.

For ChatGPT Business customers, this is a no-cost onboarding resource for staff who are just getting started. Employees who feel competent and confident using ChatGPT drive higher ROI on the Business subscription. If your team's adoption has been slower than you'd like, OpenAI Academy is the entry point worth sharing.

Access it at openai.com/academy. No separate subscription required — it's available to any OpenAI account holder.

The One Thing Disappearing: GPT-4.5 Retires June 27

GPT-4.5 launched in February 2025 as OpenAI's most capable pre-GPT-5 model. It was expensive to run, slower than alternatives, and was always positioned as a bridge product. With GPT-5.5 now the standard, GPT-4.5 is being retired from ChatGPT on June 27, 2026.

Here's what that actually means for most business users: almost nothing.

GPT-4.5 was removed from the OpenAI API in July 2025 — nearly a year ago. If you were using the API with GPT-4.5, you already migrated. The ChatGPT app retirement is a different step, affecting users who had manually selected GPT-4.5 in their model settings or built Custom GPTs targeting that model specifically.

After June 27:

  • If you had GPT-4.5 selected as your active model, ChatGPT will automatically switch you to GPT-5.5
  • Existing conversations that used GPT-4.5 will continue correctly on GPT-5.5
  • Custom GPTs that were configured to use GPT-4.5 will also migrate to the default model
✓ Bottom line: For the vast majority of ChatGPT Business users, the GPT-4.5 retirement requires zero action. The automatic migration to GPT-5.5 happens on June 27 and your experience will be seamless. The only users who should double-check anything are those who explicitly configured Custom GPTs to rely on specific GPT-4.5 behavioral characteristics — check your Custom GPT system prompts before the 27th.

How to Get the Most Out of These Updates Today

Rather than a laundry list of settings to find, here's the highest-leverage order of operations based on what we're seeing across Business deployments:

  1. Connect Gmail or Outlook (Settings → Integrations). Takes 2 minutes. Saves 20+ minutes a week for anyone who writes a lot of email.
  2. Install the ChatGPT add-in for Excel or Sheets. Even if you only use it for formula help, it pays for itself on the first complex spreadsheet task.
  3. Check your admin analytics dashboard (admin panel → Analytics). Look at who's using it and who isn't. The people not using it are the ones where adoption training helps most.
  4. Share OpenAI Academy with your team. It's free and removes the "I don't know how to use this" friction that stalls adoption.
  5. Review any Custom GPTs before June 27 if you have any that were explicitly set up to use GPT-4.5.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to do anything before GPT-4.5 retires on June 27? ▼

For most users: no. If you were using ChatGPT normally without specifically selecting GPT-4.5 in your model settings, nothing changes. The one exception is if you built Custom GPTs with system prompts explicitly designed around GPT-4.5's behavior — in that case, test those Custom GPTs before June 27 to confirm they work correctly on GPT-5.5 (which they almost certainly will, since GPT-5.5 is more capable). Existing conversations automatically continue on GPT-5.5 after the retirement date.

Is ChatGPT's email integration available on the mobile app? ▼

The email sending integration (Gmail and Outlook) is currently available on the ChatGPT web app for Business, Plus, Pro, and Enterprise users. Mobile app availability is expected but has not been confirmed by OpenAI as of the June 2026 launch. Check Settings → Integrations on web to connect your email account now, and the mobile rollout should follow.

Does the ChatGPT Excel add-in work with older versions of Excel? ▼

The ChatGPT add-in for Excel works with Microsoft 365 (cloud-connected Excel) and requires an active ChatGPT Business subscription. It does not work with standalone, perpetual-license versions of Excel (like Excel 2019 or 2021 purchased as a one-time product) as those versions don't support modern add-ins through AppSource. If your organization runs Microsoft 365 Business Basic, Standard, or Premium, you're covered.

Is GPT-5.5 better than GPT-4.5 for business writing tasks? ▼

Yes, in most cases. GPT-5.5 Instant shows improved instruction-following, more consistent professional tone, and fewer factual errors than GPT-4.5 in OpenAI's internal testing (52.5% fewer hallucinations reported for the Instant variant). For business writing tasks — drafting emails, summarizing documents, generating reports, writing SOPs — GPT-5.5 generally produces better first-draft quality. Run your most common use cases and compare if you want empirical confirmation for your specific workflows.

How do I access the new admin analytics in ChatGPT Business? ▼

Log into ChatGPT with your Business workspace admin credentials and navigate to the Admin panel (top right menu → Admin Console). The Analytics section shows per-user usage data, feature adoption, and usage trends. The updated Spend Controls section (for API usage associated with your organization) is in the same Admin Console under Billing. If you don't see the admin options, confirm that your account has admin permissions — your workspace owner can grant this access.
