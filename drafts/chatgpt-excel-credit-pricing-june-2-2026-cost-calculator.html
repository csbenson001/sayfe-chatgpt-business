What Actually Changes at Midnight Tonight

OpenAI shipped ChatGPT for Excel and Google Sheets to general availability on May 5, 2026 with a free preview window for paid plans. The published Help Center note is direct: “Business, Enterprise, Edu, and K-12 customers have a free preview through June 2, 2026; after that, usage follows each plan’s credits and usage terms.”

In practice, three things change tomorrow morning:

  1. The add-in keeps working. You do not lose access. The toolbar in Excel and the sidebar in Google Sheets still launch ChatGPT, and your team’s prompts and saved workflows are preserved.
  2. Every task draws credits from your plan’s monthly pool. Each spreadsheet task is metered separately from chat. Hit your monthly credit allotment and you either purchase more credits (Business admins can do this) or wait until the next cycle.
  3. Pricing is token-based, not flat-fee. A “task” consumes credits based on the actual mix of input tokens, cached input tokens, and output tokens. That is why OpenAI’s public range is 5–30 credits per task — the real number depends on how much data you hand the model.

How the Credit Math Actually Works

Credits in ChatGPT Business work like prepaid minutes on an old cell plan. Each Business seat ships with a monthly allotment of flex credits, and any feature that runs “on top of” the chat interface — Codex, Agents, Excel/Sheets — pulls from the same pool. (For the official rate breakdown see OpenAI’s ChatGPT Rate Card for Business and Enterprise.)

Three variables drive the credit cost of any one ChatGPT for Excel or Sheets task:

  • Input tokens. Roughly equivalent to “how many cells of data did you pass in.” A 200-row CSV is cheap. A 50,000-row sales export is expensive.
  • Cached input tokens. If you re-run the same prompt against the same data, OpenAI charges a discounted rate on the repeated input. Caching is the #1 reason “the same task” can cost very different amounts on different days.
  • Output tokens. “Rewrite this column” produces less output than “generate a 3-tab forecast with formulas and notes,” and you pay for what comes back out of the model.

To make the rest of this post useful, here is the mental model we are going to use:

Three credit tiers for ChatGPT for Excel/Sheets:
• Light (5–10 credits) — small data in, small data out. Reformat a column, draft a single formula, summarize a 50-row table.
• Medium (10–20 credits) — mid-size data, structured output. Clean a customer list, classify 1,000 rows, build a pivot, write a 3-paragraph summary on a sheet.
• Heavy (20–30+ credits) — large data, multi-step output. Multi-tab forecast, full P&L rebuild, classify-then-summarize across a 10K-row dataset.

4 Tactics to Cut Your Credit Burn by 60% Starting Tonight

You do not have to wait for the first invoice to find out you over-budgeted. Four habits will keep your team in the Light/Medium tier almost regardless of volume:

1. Filter, then send to ChatGPT

The single biggest credit-driver is “how much data did you hand the model.” If you only need a sentiment score on this week’s 200 customer reviews, do not point the add-in at a 50,000-row historical archive. Use a normal Excel filter or a Sheets pivot to narrow first — then prompt.

2. Re-use the same prompt template against the same data

OpenAI’s caching gives you a real discount when you run the same prompt against the same input. That means if your operations lead is going to classify support tickets every Monday, “save and re-run” is meaningfully cheaper than “write a fresh prompt every time.” Standardize the prompts your team uses for recurring jobs.

3. Push the “think” into the chat, the “do” into the sheet

Brainstorming the structure of a forecast, the rubric for a lead score, or the columns you want in your cleaned customer list is free under your existing ChatGPT seat. Once the structure is locked, switch to Excel/Sheets to execute it on data. Doing your “thinking” in the chat interface and your “doing” in the spreadsheet is the single fastest credit-saver.

4. Set a monthly credit budget per team

Business admins can see usage and purchase credits inside the admin console. Set explicit monthly credit budgets for finance, sales, and ops — then review them on the same cadence you review software spend. Treating credits as a real line item is the difference between “AI is cheap” and “AI is unpredictable.”

Frequently Asked Questions

Exactly when does the free preview end? ▼

OpenAI’s published guidance is that the free preview for Business, Enterprise, Edu, and K-12 customers runs through June 2, 2026. In practice that means usage on or after June 2 starts drawing credits from your plan’s monthly pool. Tasks run before that timestamp remain free.

How much will a typical small business team spend per month? ▼

For most 5–10 person SMB teams running standard finance, sales, and ops workflows, the included credit allotment on a ChatGPT Business seat covers monthly Excel/Sheets usage with room to spare. Heavy spreadsheet users — finance ops, RevOps, analytics teams — should plan on purchasing top-up credits, typically the equivalent of one extra seat per month.

Does the chat-based ChatGPT inside my Business seat still cost zero per task? ▼

Yes. Standard chat usage on a ChatGPT Business seat is included with your subscription and does not draw credits. Only specific add-on features — ChatGPT for Excel/Sheets, ChatGPT Agents, and Codex — consume credits beyond the seat fee. Doing your thinking in chat and your spreadsheet execution in the add-in is the cheapest workflow.

Can I disable ChatGPT for Excel for my team to avoid credit burn? ▼

Yes. Business admins can manage feature availability from the admin console and can set credit budgets per team or per user. Most teams should not turn it off — the value is too high — but you can scope access (for example, enable only for finance and ops) until you have observed usage patterns for a month.

What happens if my team blows through the monthly credit pool? ▼

Business admins can purchase additional credits in-app. There is no service outage — you simply pay-as-you-go for incremental usage. Standard chat continues to work at no per-task cost. The smart move is to set a credit budget you are comfortable with and review usage weekly during the first month under the new pricing.
